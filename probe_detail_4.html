back Rover
Scientists have now come up with a way to release a rover onto a planet from a lander spacecraft. These rovers are just like remote-control cars which can be steered from Earth.
The rovers can give scientists a great view of the planet by driving over rocks and valleys to take pictures and conduct soil experiments. The probes can then send the data back to Earth.
A rover called Sojourner was first released onto Mars from the Pathfinder lander in 1997. Spirit stopped operating in 2010 and Opportunity is still operating today.
