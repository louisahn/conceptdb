back The Electromagnetic Spectrum
There are many kinds of these electromagnetic waves, all arranged on a “spectrum.” The different types of waves on the spectrum all have different lengths. The waves of some are short and others are long.
From the longest wavelength to the shortest, these electromagnetic waves include: radio waves, microwaves, infrared, optical, ultraviolet, X-rays, and gamma rays.
Among the different electromagnetic waves, some we can see and others we cannot. For example, we can see light waves. This part of the electromagnetic spectrum gives us the colors we see in the rainbow. However, we cannot see gamma-rays, microwaves, or X-rays.
Electromagnetic waves, including radio waves, can travel in a vaccuum where there is no air. As you know, space is a vaccuum. This means that radio waves can travel in space!
Radio waves travel at the same speed as light--that's a lot faster than sound travels! Lightning and thunder illustrate this difference in speed. First you see lightning, then you hear thunder. Although both start at the same time, the light gets to us before the sound.
